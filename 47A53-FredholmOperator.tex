A \textbf{Fredholm operator} is a bounded operator between Banach spaces that has a finite dimensional kernel and cokernel (and closed range).
Equivalently, it is invertible modulo compact operators.
That is, if $F\colon X \to Y$ is a Fredholm operator between two vector spaces $X$ and $Y$,
then there exists a bounded operator $G\colon Y \to X$ such that
\begin{equation}
GF-\identity_X \in \Kset(X), \quad FG-\identity_Y \in \Kset(Y),
\end{equation}
where $\Kset(X)$ denotes the space of compact operators on $X$.
(Another way to say this is that $F$ is invertible in the Calkin algebra).
The set of Fredholm operators $\{F\colon X \to X\}$ is an open subset of the Banach algebra of bounded operators $\{T\colon X \to X\}$.

If $F$ is Fredholm then so is its adjoint, $F^*$.
If $T \in \Kset(X,Y)$ is a compact operator then $F+T$ is also Fredholm.
